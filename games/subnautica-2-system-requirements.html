Subnautica 2 system requirements

Are you prepared to dive straight into Subnautica 2? Before you put on your virtual wetsuit, let's talk about what your PC requires to ensure a perfect dive. We will break down the system requirements, provide some helpful guidance, and answer frequently asked questions to prepare you for your underwater adventure.

Recognizing Subnautica 2's System Requirements

Make sure your rig is capable of handling this journey before we set sail. Here are the suggested and absolute minimum requirements:

Minimum System Requirements

  • System software: Windows 10 or later
  • Processor: AMD Ryzen 5 2600 (6 cores) or Intel Core i5-8400
  • RAM: 8 GB
  • Graphics card: NVIDIA GeForce GTX 1660 or AMD RX 5500 XT
  • DirectX: Version 12
  • Storage: 20 GB of space available
  • Network: Broadband-speed internet connection

Recommended System Requirements

  • System software: Windows 10 or later
  • Processor: AMD Ryzen 7 7700X (8 cores) or Intel Core i7-13700
  • RAM: 16 GB
  • Graphics card: AMD Radeon RX 5600 XT or NVIDIA GeForce RTX 2060
  • DirectX: Version 12
  • Storage: 20 GB of free space
  • Network: Internet connection via broadband

The Significance of These Specifications

"Do I really need all that horsepower?" you may be wondering. Let's dissect it:

  • Processor (CPU): Your computer's brain. Even under pressure, a faster CPU guarantees that the game runs smoothly.
  • Memory (RAM): RAM is the short-term memory of your computer. Faster loading times and better multitasking are made possible by more RAM.
  • Graphics (GPU): The graphics card, or GPU, is in charge of all the visual elements. The underwater world seems fantastic with little delay thanks to a strong GPU.
  • Storage: Putting the game on an SSD will significantly speed up loading times, even if 20 GB isn't much.

Getting the Most Out of Your Gaming Experience

Take into account the following advice to get the most of Subnautica 2:

  1. Update your drivers: Make sure the drivers for your GPU are current. Patches are often released by manufacturers to improve performance in new games.
  2. If you're experiencing latency, change the in-game settings:
    • Reduce the resolution to lessen the strain on your GPU.
    • Modify graphics settings like anti-aliasing, textures, and shadows for better performance.
  3. Track the Performance of Your System: Monitor system temperature and performance to reduce overheating and extend hardware lifespan.
