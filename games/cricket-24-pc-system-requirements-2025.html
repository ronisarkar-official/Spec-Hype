Cricket 24: What’s New for PC Players?

Cricket 24 takes cricket gaming to a whole new level with revamped stadium lighting, enhanced player animations, career mode improvements, and real-world tournament licensing. Unlike earlier versions, this installment is much more hardware-demanding—especially for high-FPS competitive gaming. Let’s break down exactly what you need to run Cricket 24 smoothly.

Minimum System Requirements (2024)

  • OS: Windows 10/11 (64-bit)
  • CPU: Intel Core i3-2100 or AMD FX-6300
  • RAM: 8 GB DDR3
  • GPU: NVIDIA GeForce GTX 770 (2GB VRAM)
  • Storage: 60 GB HDD or basic SSD

This setup is only ideal for users playing on 720p resolution at low settings. Frame rates may hover between 30–40 FPS with minimal background activity.

Recommended System Requirements (2024)

  • CPU: Intel Core i5-6600K or AMD Ryzen 5 1600
  • RAM: 16 GB DDR4
  • GPU: NVIDIA GTX 1660 Ti / AMD RX 580
  • Storage: 60 GB SSD (preferably NVMe)

With this build, you’ll get around 60–75 FPS on high settings at 1080p. SSD improves map loading by nearly 50%, and 16 GB RAM helps prevent stutter during replays and career cutscenes.

Top 10 Tips to Optimize Cricket 24 Performance

  1. Set resolution to 1080p or 900p for stable FPS.
  2. Keep Texture Quality to “Medium” for GPUs with 4 GB VRAM.
  3. Use FXAA over MSAA to save GPU power while keeping edges smooth.
  4. Turn off motion blur and depth of field for a cleaner experience.
  5. Always install the game on an SSD to avoid stuttering in dynamic scenes.
  6. Update GPU drivers monthly to fix micro-stutters or shader issues.
  7. Use “Game Mode” in Windows 10/11 to dedicate resources to gameplay.
  8. Disable unnecessary services and background apps like Chrome or Spotify.
  9. Overclock safely with MSI Afterburner or Intel XTU if temperatures permit.
  10. Use MSI Afterburner’s OSD to monitor GPU usage and FPS live.

Best PC Build for Competitive or Streaming (2024)

  • CPU: Intel Core i5-12600K or Ryzen 7 5800X
  • GPU: RTX 3060 / RX 6700 XT (1080p 144+ FPS)
  • RAM: 16–32 GB DDR4 3200 MHz
  • Storage: NVMe SSD (Samsung 970 Evo or WD Black SN850)
  • Extras: Capture card (Elgato), Dual Monitor Setup, OBS Studio

This setup ensures zero frame drops even during streaming, commentary, and full-match replays with overlays. Ideal for YouTube and Twitch creators.

FAQs About Cricket 24 on PC

Q1: Can I run Cricket 24 on a laptop?
A: Yes, but only if it has a dedicated GPU (like GTX 1650 or better) and at least 8 GB RAM.
Q2: Is Cricket 24 optimized for AMD GPUs?
A: Mostly. However, NVIDIA cards still perform better in shader-heavy scenes.
Q3: Can I play Cricket 24 at 4K?
A: Yes, but only with a GPU like RTX 3070 or RX 6800 XT. Expect 60 FPS with optimized settings.
Q4: What if my game stutters despite good specs?
A: Use V-Sync, check for background processes, and ensure no thermal throttling occurs.
