Wildcard Game System Requirements

Wildcard is a dynamic strategic brawler that fuses cinematic visuals, fantasy settings, real-time action, and card-based tactics.

Built on Unreal Engine 5, this game pushes the limits of hardware performance with high-fidelity graphics and fast-paced gameplay, requiring modern PC specifications for optimal performance.

    Game Overview and Mechanics

    In Wildcard, you play as a champion in a 2v2 collectible card action game (CCAG). Summon monsters using a strategic deck, and battle in real-time third-person arena combat. Choose your hero, build your deck, and dominate opponents in thrilling matches.

    Why System Requirements Matter

    Ensure Smooth Gameplay: Avoid stuttering or lag that ruins the experience.

    Prevent Compatibility Issues: Don’t waste time downloading massive files only to discover your PC can’t run the game.

    Optimize Performance: Understand specs to reduce crashes and increase stability.

    Wildcard Official System Requirements

    System requirements may change with future updates.

    Minimum Requirements

    • OS: Windows 10 (64-bit)
    • CPU: Intel Core i5-8400 / AMD Ryzen 3
    • RAM: 8 GB
    • GPU: NVIDIA GTX 1060 / AMD RX 580
    • DirectX: Version 12
    • Storage: 50 GB HDD/SSD

    Recommended Requirements

    • OS: Windows 10/11 (64-bit)
    • CPU: Intel Core i7-9700K / AMD Ryzen 5 5600X
    • RAM: 16 GB
    • GPU: NVIDIA RTX 3060 / AMD RX 6700 XT
    • DirectX: Version 12
    • Storage: SSD with 50 GB free space

    Detailed Hardware Requirements

    CPU

    While the i5-8400 can handle basic gameplay, high-intensity fights demand more power. Ryzen 5 or i7 (9th Gen+) can push 100+ FPS on medium-high settings.

    GPU

    GTX 1060 and RX 580 support 1080p at low settings. For Ultra settings, Ray Tracing, and 1440p/4K, aim for RTX 3060/3070 or RX 6800+.

    RAM

    8 GB is the bare minimum. 16 GB is ideal for smooth multitasking, faster asset loading, and future-proofing.

    Storage

    Expect the base game to use 50 GB, but keep at least 100 GB free for future patches and DLC. An SSD is highly recommended for faster load times.

    Operating System Compatibility

    Best Options: Wildcard performs best on Windows 10/11 (64-bit).

    Unsupported Systems: Older Windows versions may cause security and performance issues.

    Linux/Mac: No official support. Emulation (Proton/Wine) may work but is not guaranteed.

    Online Multiplayer and Internet Needs

    Bandwidth: At least 15 Mbps recommended for online play or streaming.

    Low Latency: Competitive play demands a low-ping, stable connection. Ethernet is preferred over Wi-Fi.

    Laptop vs Desktop Performance

    Desktops: Offer better thermal management, upgradability, and consistent performance.

    Laptops: Often throttle under prolonged gaming due to heat and power limitations—even with similar GPUs.

    Performance Optimization Tips

    Game Settings: Set shadows to low, disable motion blur, and test "Medium" settings for balance.

    System Tweaks: Update drivers, close background tasks, and activate Windows Game Mode for smoother gameplay.

    Check Your Compatibility

    Tools: Use platforms like "Can You Run It" (SystemRequirementsLab) for auto-checks.

    Manual Check: Find specs in Task Manager > Performance and Device Manager under Display Adapters.

    FAQs

    Q1: Can Wildcard run on 4GB of RAM?
    No. Even for moderate usage, 4GB is inadequate; 8GB is the absolute least, and 16GB is highly suggested.
    Q2: Can Wildcard support ultrawide monitors?
    Yes, it supports native 21:9 and 32:9 resolutions, which increases the immersive experience.
    Q3: Will Wildcard be available on consoles?
    While the game is now solely accessible on PC, console versions may be announced following its debut.
    Q4: Is a gaming laptop acceptable for Wildcard?
    While mid- to high-end gaming laptops with sufficient cooling can handle Wildcard, desktops outperform them overall.
    Q5: Do I require a graphics card, or will the inbuilt graphics work?
    Integrated graphics, such as Intel UHD or Vega 8, will not cut it. A dedicated GPU is necessary for even playable performance.
